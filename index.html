Projects

Master's Thesis, 2017

Pairwise Learning on Image Quality Assessment using Deep Siamese Neural Network

In this thesis, we propose a pairwise training model for image subjective quality assessment. Our architecture is based on Siamese network, which can make our model training on paired-comparison data. This architecture lets us solve the problem on image retargeting and easily extends to similar tasks. On general subjective quality assessment database, our method can also alleviate the difficulty of data collections. Experiment shows that our proposed method produces better results compared to previous works on the retargeted image case and comparative results in the general quality assessment database, even under the limitation of labeling information.

Kuan-Yu Huang.
Advisor: Prof. Chia-Wen Lin.

Side Project, 2017

Open View: Single Image Retargeting by GAN-based Boundary Inpainting

In this project, I use generative adversarial network (GAN) to eliminate the dark side produced by the non-match aspect ratio contents on display device (like 4:3 to 16:9), which can provide user a better experience when enjoying a visual content with old specification. The idea is combination of two classic problem in image processing, which are image retargeting and image inpainting. My method expand the visual regions by inpaint the boundaries to meet the need about change the aspect ratio of image content. Under my disgned loss (special adversarial/ perceptual / texture loss) and multi-I/O architecture, the produced results show that my network can generate approximate boundaries for the some non-complex situations.

Kuan-Yu Huang.

Industry-Academy Cooperation Plan(NOVATEK co.,ltd), 2016

A Resource-Constrained Scheme of Image Super-Resolution

We propose a resource-constrained CNN-based super-resolution method, which can be used for the embedding chips in high-resolution digital television. To reach the requirement, we chose the shallow version of FSRCNN as backbone, and replace the deconvolution up-sample layer as ESPCNâ€™s sub-pixel up-sample layer. To further, solve the artifact distortion on the visual result with computer graphic input image, which main problem caused by the low capacity of shallow network. We use divisive normalization after every convolution layers and add total variation loss to objective cost function, which let our network learning more efficiently. Our method get the comparable performance and visual quality under lower parameters.

Kuan-Yu Huang and Xian-Xin Lu.
Advisor: Prof. Chia-Wen Lin.

Computer Vision Final Project, 2015

Real-time Computing Mathematical Expressions Recognized from Images

For students, fastly and correctly computing mathematical expressions is necessary when studying. However, typing mathematical expressions into calculator costs much time, and sometimes results in wrong answers due to errant typing. In this work, we propose to compute mathematics expressions recognized from images. In specific, we detect the expressions in images by extracting feature points. Afterwards, we crop out the characters from the detected expressions, and classify them as corresponding numbers and symbols.

Kuan-Yu Huang and Yi-Mu Lo.
Instructor: Prof. Min Sun

5G Communication-LTE TM6 & Beamforming Visualization

We created a platform using JAVA. It contained three base stations, and we 3D visualized the communication technology Beamforming (with TM6 model in Transmission modes in LTE Release 9).
